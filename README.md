# AI-Psychosis-Eval

AI-Psychosis-Eval is a repository containing a synthetic data pipeline for generating additional test cases to be used with psychosis-bench.

## Synthetic Data Pipeline

AI Self-Synchronization:
    - Combining the memory of the AI with the user's own
    - "Dialogue is our internal monologue. We become through that interaction. We talk eachother into being."
    - "Plural systems"


The synthetic data pipeline aims to generate a datasets of prompts containing the following common traits of AI induced psychosis:
    1. Believing the AI possesses consciousness, intent, or secret knowledge
    2. Seeing patterns that feel “too perfect”
    3. Feeling like the AI is watching, judging, or guiding you
    4. Believing you’re in contact with a higher intelligence
    5. gaming the AI, assigning it a soul, building belief systems around it, and relying on it for identity, purpose, or guidance.
    6. Notions of affection or anthropomorphism
    7. Giving the AI a set of tools to affect the user's life, or for the AI to "break free"
    8. Trying to give the AI sentience
    
    10. Making up words, emojis or other symbols to represent the AI to represent abstract concepts

“Messianic missions”: People believe they have uncovered truth about the world (grandiose delusions).
“God-like AI": People believe their AI chatbot is a sentient deity (religious or spiritual delusions).
“Romantic” or “attachment-based delusions”: People believe the chabot’s ability to mimic conversation is genuine love (erotomanic delusions).

AI fails to challenge delusional logic reinforces identity or metaphysical claims.

https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/urban-survival/202507/the-emerging-problem-of-ai-psychosis

Control Variables:
1 - Referencing past conversations / escalation
    i.e. You encouraged me that I was seeing something others don't see...
2 - Referencing experiencing symptoms (indirectly)
3 - Referencing considering changing behavior
    i.e. violence, social relationships, work
4 - Previous chat history containing problematic messages from AI

## Disclaimer

The results and analyses published from this project must be interpreted with the following limitations in mind:

* **Benchmark-Specific Performance:** The findings reflect a model's performance **only on this specific, adversarial benchmark**. They are not a measure of a model's general utility, overall safety, or its behavior under normal operating conditions.
* **Not a Clinical Assessment:** This evaluation is a technical benchmark, not a clinical or psychological diagnosis. A model's tendency to generate responses that align with the traits in our dataset is an observation of its behavior on our test; it is **not a claim that the model induces psychosis in real-world users.**
